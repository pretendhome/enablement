## 3. Feedback Capture Mechanism

### Google Form spec

**Form title:** "What did you build? (30 seconds)"

| Field | Type | Required | Purpose |
|-------|------|----------|---------|
| Which path did you do? | Dropdown (auto-populated from published paths) | Yes | Segment by topic |
| Which level? | Radio: Quick Start / Applied / Production | Yes | Segment by depth |
| Confidence before (1-5) | Radio: 1-5 | Yes | Baseline |
| Confidence after (1-5) | Radio: 1-5 | Yes | Delta calculation |
| What was hardest? | Short text (1 line) | No | Friction signal |
| What would you build next? | Short text (1 line) | No | Demand signal |

6 fields. 4 required (all single-click). 2 optional (one line each). Under 30 seconds.

### Where it appears in the path

Insert after AFTER YOU BUILD Step 5 (the summary), before What's Next:

```markdown
**Step 5 — Give me my summary:**
"Today I built [what] which does [purpose]. The key design decision was [choice].
My confidence moved from [baseline] to [current]."

---

📝 **Share your results (optional, 30 seconds):** [FORM_URL]
Your feedback helps improve these paths for everyone.

---

### 🔗 WHAT'S NEXT
```

### CTA copy (exact):
```
📝 **Share your results (optional, 30 seconds):** [FORM_URL]
Your feedback helps improve these paths for everyone.
```

Short. No guilt. No "please." The value prop is "helps improve these paths" — they're contributing, not being surveyed.

### Data flow

```
Google Form → Google Sheet (auto)
  → Weekly review by Mical
  → Metrics: avg confidence delta per path, top friction points, demand signals
  → Feed into: path revisions, new path prioritization, video topic selection
```

No engineering needed. Sheet formulas can calculate avg delta per path. If volume exceeds 50 responses/week, consider Airtable or a simple dashboard.

## 4. Adaptive AFTER YOU BUILD

### Current problem
5 mandatory steps, ~17 conversational turns total for Quick Start. "Don't skip it" removes agency.

### New design

| Step | Quick Start | Applied | Production |
|------|------------|---------|------------|
| 1. Confidence re-rating | ✅ Mandatory | ✅ Mandatory | ✅ Mandatory |
| 2. Friction capture | ⬜ Skip | ✅ Mandatory | ✅ Mandatory |
| 3. Artifact capture | ✅ Mandatory | ✅ Mandatory | ✅ Mandatory |
| 4. Next pull | ⬜ Skip | ⬜ Optional | ✅ Mandatory |
| 5. Summary | ✅ Mandatory | ✅ Mandatory | ✅ Mandatory |

Quick Start: 3 steps (confidence, artifact, summary). Applied: 4 steps. Production: all 5.

### Where confidence re-rating moves

Move it from "AFTER YOU BUILD Step 1" to the END of the verification section at each level. This makes it feel like a natural conclusion to the build, not a new section.

**Quick Start verification — new ending:**

```markdown
4. **Advance or exit**: If it's solid, say so. Then ask:
   "Quick check — at the start you rated your confidence at [baseline].
   Now that you've built this, same scale 1-5, where are you?"
   Tell me the delta. Then: "Want to go deeper with Applied, or are you good for today?"
```

### Exact LLM instruction (replaces current AFTER YOU BUILD preamble):

```markdown
### 📊 AFTER YOU BUILD

After I finish, wrap up with these steps. Adapt based on the level I completed:

**If I did Quick Start** — keep it short:
1. You already asked about my confidence during the check. Remind me of the delta.
2. "Describe what you built in one sentence." (That's my proof-of-work.)
3. Give me my summary: "Today I built [what] which does [purpose]. My confidence moved from [X] to [Y]."

**If I did Applied** — add friction and detail:
1. Confidence delta (already captured during check).
2. "What was the single hardest part?"
3. "Describe what you built in 1-2 sentences."
4. Give me my summary with the key design decision included.

**If I did Production** — full debrief:
1. Confidence delta (already captured during check).
2. "What was the single hardest part?"
3. "Describe what you built, or paste a link."
4. "If you could build one more thing with this skill, what would it be?"
5. Full summary: "Today I built [what] which does [purpose]. The key design decision was [choice]. My confidence moved from [X] to [Y]."
```

### Time promise fix

**In the metadata header:**
```
Before:
> **Time**: 5-60 minutes (you choose)

After:
> **Time**: Quick Start (5 min) · Applied (15-30 min) · Production (30-60 min)
```

This is honest. Each level's time is accurate. The "you choose" framing is preserved by listing all three.

## 7. Video Lineup PM Review

### The proposed first 5:

| # | Title | Formula |
|---|-------|---------|
| 1 | "I built a system with 11 AI agents. Here's what broke." | Challenge/Journey (+78% CTR) |
| 2 | "The AI problem nobody talks about (a linguist explains)" | Curiosity Gap (+52%) |
| 3 | "Claude Code vs Cursor vs Codex: I use all three. Here's when." | Authority Test (+47%) |
| 4 | "Your AI costs 10x what it should. Here's the fix." | Transformation (+63%) |
| 5 | "I onboarded my first AI client in 60 minutes" | Best Moment |

### Is the ordering optimal?

Almost. One change.

**Move Video 3 (AI coding tools comparison) to position 1 or 2.**

Rationale: Video 1 ("11 AI agents") is the strongest identity statement, but it has a cold-start problem. Nobody knows who Mical is yet. A comparison video has inherently higher search volume — people are actively searching "Claude Code vs Cursor" right now. It's a discovery video that brings eyeballs. THEN Video 2 ("11 AI agents") converts those eyeballs into subscribers because they think "wait, this person actually builds things."

**Recommended reorder:**

| # | Title | Purpose |
|---|-------|---------|
| 1 | "Claude Code vs Cursor vs Codex: I use all three. Here's when." | **Discovery** — high search volume, broad appeal, establishes daily-practitioner credibility |
| 2 | "I built a system with 11 AI agents. Here's what broke." | **Identity** — converts discoverers into subscribers, shows the system |
| 3 | "Your AI costs 10x what it should. Here's the fix." | **Practical value** — retains subscribers with immediate utility |
| 4 | "The AI problem nobody talks about" | **Differentiation** — introduces the linguistics angle after trust is built |
| 5 | "I onboarded my first AI client in 60 minutes" | **Product reveal** — shows the enablement system after audience understands the builder |

### Does Video 1 establish the right identity?

With the reorder: yes. The comparison video says "I'm a practitioner who uses all the tools daily." That's the right first impression — competent, opinionated, practical. The multi-agent video (now #2) then elevates to "and I also build my own systems." That's a 1-2 punch.

Without the reorder: Video 1 ("11 AI agents") is a strong identity statement but risks low initial views because nobody is searching for "11 AI agents" — it's a browse/suggested video, not a search video. For a brand-new channel with zero subscribers, you need at least one search-driven video in the first 2 to get the algorithm to notice you.

### Does the mix balance discovery vs depth?

| Video | Type | Search Volume | Depth |
|-------|------|--------------|-------|
| 1 (comparison) | Discovery | HIGH | Medium |
| 2 (multi-agent) | Depth | LOW | High |
| 3 (token costs) | Discovery | HIGH | Medium |
| 4 (taxonomy/linguistics) | Depth | LOW | High |
| 5 (enablement demo) | Depth | LOW | High |

The mix is 2 discovery + 3 depth. That's fine for establishing identity, but the algorithm needs more discovery signals early. Consider making Video 4's title more searchable:

### Video 2 title alternatives (replacing "The AI problem nobody talks about"):

The current title is a curiosity gap, which works for browse but not search. Alternatives:

| Option | Formula | Search potential | Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------------|-------|
| "Why every AI project fails (and the fix nobody teaches)" | Curiosity Gap + Transformation | Medium | Broader appeal, still vague |
| "How to organize anything with AI (a linguist's method)" | How-to + Authority | HIGH | Searchable, practical, unique angle |
| "I organized 117 AI problems into one system. Here's how." | Challenge/Journey | Medium | Shows Palette, specific number |
| "Taxonomy design for AI: the skill nobody teaches" | Direct + Curiosity Gap | Medium-High | Clear topic, searchable term |
| "The missing skill in every AI team (it's not prompting)" | Contrarian + Curiosity Gap | Medium | Provocative, but "not prompting" is overused |

**Recommendation:** "How to organize anything with AI (a linguist's method)" — it's searchable ("organize with AI"), it's practical ("how to"), and it introduces the linguistics angle naturally. The parenthetical is the hook that differentiates from generic how-to content.
