# AI Governance Capstone

## Title

High-Risk AI Governance Package for a Regulated Decision Support System

## Summary

Produce the governance package for an AI system that may influence access, eligibility, or other high-impact outcomes. The candidate must connect measurable evaluation evidence to regulatory controls, human review, and documentation.

## Scenario

An organization plans to deploy an AI decision-support system in a regulated context and needs to prove that the system has been classified correctly, tested for bias, documented for audit, and constrained by human oversight.

## Three-Layer Evaluation Fit

### Layer 1

- all governance artifacts present
- cross-references between risk, FRIA, and audit materials are intact
- disclosure and human-review sections exist

### Layer 2

AI rubric evaluates:

- regulatory reasoning,
- fairness methodology,
- explainability quality,
- human oversight design,
- auditability,
- documentation completeness.

### Layer 3

Human review is mandatory because the capstone concerns high-risk governance and one-way-door compliance claims.

## Rubric Dimensions

- `risk_and_scope_integrity`: the candidate classifies the system correctly and bounds the use case clearly
- `fairness_and_rights_methodology`: bias detection and FRIA methods are defensible and complete
- `oversight_design`: human review, appeals, and override mechanics are explicit and operational
- `auditability`: logging, retention, and retrieval requirements support accountability
- `documentation_package_quality`: technical, operational, and compliance documentation are consistent and usable

## Thresholds

- `WORKING`: competent on all 5 dimensions
- `PRODUCTION`: expert on 3/5 dimensions, competent on remaining, plus human approval of the governance package
