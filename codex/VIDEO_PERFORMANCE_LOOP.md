# Video Performance Loop
**Purpose**: A reusable loop for recorded artifacts such as Looms, demos, onboarding walkthroughs, and executive briefings.

## Why This Needs A Separate Loop

A person can:
- know the content
- pass live Q&A
- still fail the recording

Recorded artifacts add different constraints:
- time cap
- pacing
- transitions
- visual sequencing
- recovery from glitches
- executive presence

## The Loop

### Step 1: Lock the claim set

Before recording, identify:
- the core thesis
- the must-hit lines
- the claims that are safe
- the claims that must not drift

### Step 2: Build the narrative spine

The artifact should have:
1. opening hook
2. problem framing
3. operating model
4. live proof
5. measured close

If any section is optional, define it before recording.

### Step 3: Rehearse for timing

Measure:
- total runtime
- slowest transition
- highest-risk live segment

Do not add content when over time.
Swap and compress.

### Step 4: Rehearse for trust

Remove:
- playful lines that lower executive trust
- vague claims
- filler phrasing
- product mythology

Keep:
- warmth
- human phrasing
- operator confidence

### Step 5: Rehearse failure recovery

Plan for:
- weak output
- slow output
- wrong window
- wrong wording
- time overrun

### Step 6: Record multiple takes

Take 1:
- pacing

Take 2:
- clarity

Take 3:
- only if a real issue remains

### Step 7: Grade the artifact

Score separately:
- content quality
- demo coherence
- delivery trust
- elimination risk

## What I Would Do Differently From The Start

If I were starting over, I would have separated:
- content coaching
- performance coaching

much earlier.

That would have reduced drift between:
- what sounded good in writing
- what survived the time cap
- what still felt credible on video

## Best Practices

- choose one workflow, not three
- make the demo reinforce the operating model
- do not let supporting visuals become the star
- keep the close decision-oriented

## Reusable Evaluation Questions

1. What is the single idea the viewer should remember?
2. What proof makes that idea credible?
3. What part of the artifact would break first under pressure?
4. What can be removed without changing the thesis?
5. What line weakens trust more than it adds personality?
